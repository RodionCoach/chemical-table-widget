Living organisms are classified into categories by their characteristics. On the following pages of this journal, you will see some of the basic characteristics used to classify organisms.
Prokaryotic cells are simple cells with no nucleus. The genetic information within a prokaryotic cell is free floating.
Eukaryotic cells are complex cells with many internal structures. The most important characteristic of a eukaryotic cell is the presence of a membrane-bound nucleus that contains the genetic information.
Unicellular organisms are living organisms that are each composed of one single cell.
Multicellular organisms are living organisms made of many cells. These cells work together to allow an organism to function.
Autotrophic organisms make their own nutrients. Through processes such as photosynthesis, these organisms can produce their own food for survival.
Heterotrophic organisms cannot make their own nutrients. These organisms must consume other organisms (either autotrophic or heterotrophic) to gain nutrients needed for survival.
